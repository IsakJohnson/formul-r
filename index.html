Gamla testamentet

Isaiah 53

Isaiah 53:1-12 - "Who has believed our message and to whom has the arm of the LORD been revealed? He grew up before him like a tender shoot, and like a root out of dry ground. He had no beauty or majesty to attract us to him, nothing in his appearance that we should desire him. He was despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering, and familiar with pain. Like one from whom people hide their faces he was despised, and we held him in low esteem. Surely he took up our pain and bore our suffering, yet we considered him punished by God, stricken by him, and afflicted. But he was pierced for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on him, and by his wounds we are healed. We all, like sheep, have gone astray, each of us has turned to our own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all. He was oppressed and afflicted, yet he did not open his mouth; he was led like a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before its shearers is silent, so he did not open his mouth. By oppression and judgment he was taken away. Yet who of his generation protested? For he was cut off from the land of the living; for the transgression of my people he was punished. He was assigned a grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death, though he had done no violence, nor was any deceit in his mouth. Yet it was the LORD's will to crush him and cause him to suffer, and though the LORD makes his life an offering for sin, he will see his offspring and prolong his days, and the will of the LORD will prosper in his hand. After he has suffered, he will see the light of life and be satisfied; by his knowledge my righteous servant will justify many, and he will bear their iniquities. Therefore I will give him a portion among the great, and he will divide the spoils with the strong, because he poured out his life unto death, and was numbered with the transgressors. For he bore the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors."

Isaiah 53 är en tydlig profetia som refererar till Jesus som Messias, många århundraden före hans födelse. Texten beskriver en tjänare som lider och dör för andras synder, vilket tydligt pekar på Jesus’ uppdrag och hans försonande död. Det är viktigt att notera att han blev avvisad och dödad av sitt eget folk, judarna, vilket framhäver den tragiska ironi i hans liv. Vidare bekräftar många andra gamla testamentet verser hans gudomliga identitet, som i Isaiah 9:6 - "For to us a child is born, to us a son is given; and the government shall be upon his shoulder, and his name shall be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace." Denna profetia har uppfyllts på ett sätt som gör att vi inte kan se den som något annat än en direkt förutsägelse om Jesus och hans gudomliga roll i vår frälsning.

Nya testamentet

Helheten

John 1:1-3 - "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. He was with God in the beginning. Through him all things were made; without him nothing was made that has been made."

Johannesevangeliet inleder med att hävda att Jesus är Gud, vilket är en central tro i kristendomen. Det är genom Jesus som skapelsen sker, och han är inte bara en människa utan den gudomliga inkarnationen. Att förstå Jesus på detta sätt är avgörande för vår tro. Hans död på korset och den följande uppståndelsen är inte bara en historisk händelse, utan en grundpelare för vår frälsning, som kopplar oss tillbaka till den förutsagda Messias i Isaiah 53. Jesus sade också: John 11:25 - "I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die." Vi ser också att han dödades av sina egna, vilket var en del av Guds plan för att föra oss till försoning med Honom.

Koranen

Jesus är betraktad som Gud

Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:110) - "The Day when Allah will say, 'O Jesus, remember My favor upon you and upon your mother when I supported you with the Pure Spirit and you spoke to the people in the cradle and in maturity; and [remember] when I taught you writing and wisdom and the Torah and the Gospel; and when you design from clay [what is] like the form of a bird, then breathe into it, and it becomes a bird by permission of Allah...'"

Koranen erkänner att Jesus utförde mirakler, såsom att ge liv åt lera och få den att bli en fågel, något som bara Gud kan göra. Dessutom helade han sjuka och uppväckte de döda, som nämnts i Surah Al-Imran (3:49) - "And [make him] a messenger to the Children of Israel, [who will say], 'Indeed, I have come to you with a sign from your Lord, in that I design for you from clay [what is] like the form of a bird; then I breathe into it, and it becomes a bird by permission of Allah. And I heal the blind and the leper and I bring the dead to life by permission of Allah...'" och Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:110) - "And [the Day] when Allah will say, 'O Jesus, remember My favor upon you and upon your mother when I supported you with the Pure Spirit and you spoke to the people in the cradle and in maturity; and [remember] when I taught you writing and wisdom and the Torah and the Gospel; and when you design from clay [what is] like the form of a bird; then you breathe into it, and it becomes a bird by permission of Allah, and you heal the blind and the leper and bring the dead to life by My permission...'". Detta pekar på hans unika position i Guds plan. Trots att Koranen inte betraktar Jesus som Gud, erkänns hans extraordinära förmågor, vilket understryker att han hade en gudomlig aspekt. Genom att kombinera dessa synpunkter kan vi se att Jesus’ liv och gärningar i själva verket pekar mot hans gudomliga natur. Hans återkomst, som omnämns i både Koranen och Bibeln, ytterligare förstärker hans betydelse som en central figur för troende.
